Research Experience

  • Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center Seattle, WA
    Graduate Research Assistant Jul 2024 – Jul 2025
    ¡Vida Study!, Greenlee Lab
    PI: Heather Greenlee, ND, PhD, MPH

    The ¡Vida! Study is a SMART (Sequential Multiple Assignment Randomized Trial) evaluating adaptive lifestyle interventions to support Latina breast cancer survivors across California and Washington State.
    • Manage participant tracking systems across study arms using REDCap, including Fitabase and Mosio integration to monitor Fitbit-derived physical activity and coordinate participant communication
    • Led development of materials and protocols to support remotely delivered intervention components, including structured account setup guides, troubleshooting workflows, and participant training for Fitbit and messaging systems
    • Contribute to digital infrastructure decision-making, fidelity monitoring, and feasibility assessments of remote data collection strategies
    • Train research interns on study protocol procedures, including REDCap data management, Fitbit device setup, Fitabase system integration, and preparation of participant-facing study materials
  • University of Washington Seattle, WA
    Graduate Research Assistant Jul 2022 – Jul 2024
    CoLab for Community and Behavioral Health, Department of Psychiatry and Behavioral Health
    PI: Sarah Walker, PhD, MSc

    CoLab engages community expertise and research evidence to spark creative and sustainable change in behavioral health policy and systems, particularly those involving youth and families.
    • Co-authored a manuscript using cascade analysis to evaluate the implementation and service delivery efficiency of a cash-plus intervention designed to connect youth experiencing homelessness with social services
    • Conducted statistical analyses—including logistic regression and bivariate tests—for H-SYNC, an alternative-to-detention initiative; led data cleaning and preparation for publication and stakeholder dissemination
    • Collaborated with community stakeholders in Pierce County, WA, to design a trauma-informed afterschool program for system-involved youth
    • Presented findings and facilitated discussions in cross-sector meetings with local leaders, service providers, and researchers to translate findings into practice
  • University of Washington Seattle, WA
    Graduate Research Assistant Jan 2022 – Jul 2022
    Amigas Latinas Motivando el Alma (ALMA), Department of Health Systems and Population Health
    PI: India Ornelas, PhD, MPH

    ALMA is a strengths-based mental health intervention for recent Latina immigrant women, designed to reduce depression and anxiety by enhancing existing coping strategies and introducing new, culturally grounded mindfulness practices and peer-based social support.
    • Conducted statistical analyses of cross-sectional and longitudinal survey data—including bivariate analyses, chi-square tests, one-way ANOVA with Tukey’s HSD post-hoc tests, and Kruskal–Wallis tests with pairwise Wilcoxon post-hoc comparisons using R—to evaluate changes in depression, anxiety, coping, and social support
    • Led dissemination efforts including national conference presentations, peer-reviewed manuscript preparation, and reports shared with community collaborators
    • Mentored master’s-level students in translating their thesis projects into peer-reviewed manuscripts for academic publication
  • University of Washington Seattle, WA
    Graduate Research Assistant Sep 2021 – Jan 2022
    Partnership for Vaccine Confidence (PVC), Health Promotion Research Center
    PI: Barbara Baquero, PhD, MPH

    This community-partnered study aimed to improve COVID-19 vaccine confidence among Latino and immigrant multigenerational households in King County and Yakima, WA, through culturally responsive health communication and outreach strategies.
    • Conducted bilingual (Spanish/English) qualitative interviews with community members in Yakima to explore vaccine hesitancy and trusted sources of information
    • Co-developed a REDCap-based survey tool to complement qualitative data collection on COVID-19 vaccine attitudes and behaviors
    • Led a field team of undergraduate and master’s students during site visits, coordinating logistics and ensuring cultural responsiveness
    • Generated weekly reports on local vaccine rates using R; developed data visualizations and summaries for internal dissemination to Health Promotion Research Center leadership
  • National Institute of Health Ann Arbor, MI
    National Human Genome Research Institute Trainee Sep 2020 – Sep 2021
    Sterilization and Social Justice Lab (SSJL)
    PI: Alexandra Minna Stern, PhD; Supervisor: Nicole L. Novak, PhD, MSc

    The SSJL uses epidemiologic and historical methods to examine the legacy of eugenic sterilization policies in the U.S. across five states: CA, IA, MI, NC, and UT.
    • Applied linear interpolation techniques to 1920–1945 census microdata and conducted negative binomial Poisson regression using Stata to analyze the relationship between immigration-based regional origin and sterilization rates in California state records
    • Conducted archival research at the Iowa State Capitol, analyzing historical sterilization requests and developing qualitative themes on the rationale for reproductive control
    • Presented research at national conferences, including the NHGRI Trainee Research Meeting (selected as one of six oral presenters) and the ELSI Congress hosted by Columbia University
  • University of Iowa Iowa City, IA
    Graduate Research Assistant Aug 2018 – Aug 2020
    Eugenic Legacies Lab
    PI: Nicole L. Novak, PhD, MSc

    This project analyzed over 2,000 sterilization records from Iowa’s state archives (1910–1970) to create a quantitative database of coercive and involuntary sterilizations.
    • Led archival fieldwork in Iowa, collecting and coding primary documents from historical sterilization requests to support database development and demographic analysis
    • Produced a quantitative dataset of sterilization cases with linked medical, demographic, and institutional variables for historical epidemiologic research
    • Presented findings on Iowa’s eugenics history at state-level and institutional conferences, highlighting ethical and policy-driven implications
    • Mentored undergraduate students in qualitative and quantitative methods, including training on REDCap for data entry and project documentation
  • University of Iowa & University of Michigan Iowa City, IA
    Graduate Research Assistant Aug 2018 – Aug 2020
    Immigration Enforcement and Health Equity Lab
    PI’s: Nicole L. Novak, PhD, MSc and William D. Lopez, PhD

    This community-based research initiative examined the public health impacts of immigration enforcement across six U.S. states, using mixed methods and participatory approaches.
    • Conducted bilingual (Spanish/English) interviews with community leaders and directly impacted individuals following large-scale immigration raids across Iowa, Minnesota, Nebraska, Ohio, Tennessee, and Texas
    • Applied descriptive quantitative methods in R to estimate the local impact of raids on families and communities, including proportional analyses of household disruptions and detention rates
    • Participated in site visits and stakeholder meetings with churches, Iowa’s community bail fund network, and advocacy coalitions to present health harms of enforcement policies and mobilize community response strategies
    • Contributed to a forthcoming book on immigration enforcement and community resistance; featured as a central character based on embedded fieldwork and sustained collaboration with affected communities
